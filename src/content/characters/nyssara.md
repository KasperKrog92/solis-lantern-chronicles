Nyssara came to Brinegate three months ago, through the gate, with a bag that contained everything she owned and a name she was hoping no one had yet heard. The name, in her case, is a problem: her mother is Dolores Tarius, a Drow matriarch and one of the Empire's senior government ministers, and that reputation follows their bloodline with the tenacity of a good debt collector.

She appears fully Drow, but her eyes are golden — a visible inheritance from the human soldier her mother chose deliberately as her father. She conceals them behind tinted glasses, telling anyone who asks that she has a light sensitivity condition. She doesn't.

She is not running from her family so much as building something independent of it. The distinction matters to her, even if the result looks the same from the outside. She collects information carefully and shares it sparingly. She builds relationships the same way — not coldly, but deliberately, always aware that the people around her today may be exactly what she needs tomorrow. The Solis Lantern was an obvious move: a founding membership means influence, agency, and a legitimate base from which to build an information network close to the Outer Tier, where the military and other high-standing individuals move through constantly.

What she is less methodical about: why she agreed to Kisara's errand before the money was fully discussed. Why she noticed, that night in the graveyard, that Mara didn't ask for help when the vines had her — and why that particular observation settled somewhere in her memory with unusual weight.

Her mother writes often. Nyssara replies selectively. She suspects the guild will complicate her careful arrangements. She has decided this is probably acceptable.
