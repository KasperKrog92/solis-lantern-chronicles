Querk came to Brinegate looking for a person. The person — an anthropologist named Eldris Vane, who had spent fifteen years documenting the ecological effects of Leviathan-bled soil on Outer Tier vegetation — had last been heard from in a letter dated eight months before Querk's arrival, postmarked from the lower Wall district. Subsequent letters had gone unanswered. The university that employed Vane had filed the matter under *field research* and moved on.

Querk has not moved on.

They are methodical in the way that naturalists are methodical — not rigid, but patient, with an attention for detail that looks, to the untrained eye, like detachment. It is not detachment. It is a very focused kind of care, applied to everything: the soup at the inn (unusual spice blend, suggests a trade connection to the inner middle district), the vines along the Wall (Leviathan blood as a growth accelerant, manifesting as something closer to territorial aggression than simple overgrowth), the way Mara held herself when she was injured and would not show it (careful note, filed for later).

The squirrels that night were not, strictly speaking, an intentional choice. Querk opened a channel of communication with the local fauna as a precautionary measure, and the squirrels had opinions.

They are still looking for Eldris Vane.
