Querc grew up in an indigenous tribe deep in the forests of the Middle Tier, largely removed from the Empire's civilization. Their world changed when Tristan Bas, a linguist anthropologist, came to live with the tribe for several months to study their language and oral traditions. He befriended Querc, told them about the wider world, and left something unresolved between them — feelings that seemed mutual but were never spoken aloud before he returned to his research.

Two things have since gone wrong. The environment around the tribal village has shifted in ways that feel unnatural, and Querc's mother Jenevele — the tribe's matriarch — has fallen ill with a sickness that no traditional remedy can touch. Querc stayed as long as they could, helping care for her alongside their younger sister Chi-Ro. Eventually they made the difficult decision to leave, telling themselves it was to understand what was happening to the land. The quieter truth is that they also hoped to find Tristan again.

Tristan once mentioned working at a research facility called Jakata in the Outer Tier, near Brinegate. That became their destination. They arrived three months ago and have been stuck here since — indigenous people face a complicated legal status under Imperial law, and Querc is caught in a bureaucratic process to obtain citizenship before they can even apply for gate access. They joined the Solis Lantern partly for practical support, and partly because having people around makes the waiting bearable.

They are methodical in the way that naturalists are methodical — not rigid, but patient, with an attention for detail that looks, to the untrained eye, like detachment. It is not detachment. It is a very focused kind of care, applied to everything: the soup at the inn, the vines along the Wall (Leviathan blood as a growth accelerant, manifesting as something closer to territorial aggression than simple overgrowth), the way Mara held herself when she was injured and would not show it (careful note, filed for later).

The squirrels that night were not, strictly speaking, an intentional choice. Querc opened a channel of communication with the local fauna as a precautionary measure, and the squirrels had opinions.

They are still looking for Tristan Bas.
