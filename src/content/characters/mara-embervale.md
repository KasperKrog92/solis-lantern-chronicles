Mara arrived in Brinegate six months before the founding of the Solis Lantern, following a trail that had gone cold twice already. Her mother and younger sister disappeared two years prior during the last wet season — the official record lists them as casualties of a Leviathan incursion on the outer road, but Mara has never believed the official record.

She carries her faith with the practicality of someone who has tested it against difficult conditions and found it, improbably, sufficient. Her order — a small, itinerant sect that follows one of the old gods of the inner Wall districts — does not require certainty, only presence. Mara is very good at presence.

The vines along the Wall that night were the first real test of her endurance since she'd arrived in Brinegate. She passed it, in the way that matters: she didn't ask for help, but she didn't refuse it either. Nyssara and Querk pulled her free. Wynn's music held the plants. She noted all of this and filed it away.

She doesn't talk about her mother and sister much. She talks about the guild, and what it can become, and what the city needs from people like them. Whether these are the same conversation is a question she hasn't answered yet.
