# Setting

## **I. The World: A Study in Distance**

The Empire survives not by conquering the world, but by dividing it. To the east lies the sea, the birthplace of the **Leviathans** \- massive, sentient entities that will emerge from the sea and attack the Empire each “wet season”, about once a year. The whole empire has been organized for centuries around this constant threat. Civilization is organized into three tiers, separated by three Great Walls that act as the Empire's skeletal defense.

* **The Outer Tier:** The front line, very close to the sea. A landscape of military testing grounds and laboratories where scientists and soldiers develop new ways to hold back the Leviathans. Serves as the main battleground for holding back the Leviathans from the rest of the Empire. Since this is where most Leviathans die, these lands are also the most affected by Leviathan blood. “The Outer Wall” is the wall that separates the Outer Tier to the west and the Sea to the east.  
* **The Middle Tier:** Where most citizens reside and most resources of the Empire are produced. Because the military is focused on the inner tier, there is a degree of lawlessness and lack of government justice in the Middle Tier when problems are deemed not important and under-prioritized by the bureaucracy. As a consequence, adventuring parties are often formed to help out people that do not get governmental help. “The Middle Wall” is the wall that separates the Middle Tier to the west and the Outer Tier to the east.   
* **The Inner Tier:** The furthest inland. A place of relative safety where nobles and administrators govern from a distance, removed from the fight and struggles of the Middle and Outer Tier.”The Inner Wall” separates the Inner Tier to the west and the Middle Tier to the east.  
  ---

  ## **II. The Blood & The Land**

The environment has been fundamentally altered by **Leviathan Blood**. from centuries of slain Leviathans. This ichor has saturated the earth, causing nature to grow faster and stranger than in ancient times. The effects on the tiers are different:

* **The Outer Tier**  
  In the Outer Tier, leviathan blood exists in its rawest and most concentrated form, saturating the land around the colossal carcasses that lie scattered across the wasteland. The environment feels unstable and strangely alive, shaped by the slow decay of creatures so vast they resemble fallen mountains. Fungal networks spread through soil and stone, feeding on the residue of leviathan flesh, while twisted vines and parasitic plants grow in aggressive, unnatural patterns. Entire ecosystems emerge around the bodies, thriving on blood, bone, and decay. Pools of thick, dark blood sometimes gather in hollows of the land, seeping slowly into the ground and altering whatever life grows nearby. Animals that feed in these regions often change in unpredictable ways, becoming larger, stranger, or more hostile. For the harvesters, researchers, and adventurers who venture beyond the Wall, the Outer Tier is both a biological frontier and a place of profound danger, where the very landscape seems to grow from the remains of immense and unknowable beings.

* **The Middle Tier**  
  The Middle Tier is far more stable, but it is not untouched by leviathan influence. Most of the time the land resembles a familiar and livable environment of towns, roads, and cultivated fields built under the protection of the Second Wall. Yet every few years a leviathan breaches the First Wall and reaches the defenses of the Middle Tier before it is brought down. When this happens, the landscape can be profoundly altered. The body of the slain leviathan slowly leaks blood into the surrounding earth, and the area around the corpse begins to change. Strange fungal growth spreads through the soil, unfamiliar plants take root, and the land can remain marked by the creature’s presence for years afterward. Entire local ecosystems may shift as life adapts to the strange nutrients left behind. Over time these rare events leave scattered pockets of unnatural growth across the Middle Tier—places where the environment feels subtly different, as though touched by something vast and alien. These sites often become both hazards and opportunities, drawing scholars, harvesters, and opportunists eager to extract what remains of the leviathan’s strange biology.

* **The Inner Tier**  
  In the Inner Tier, the natural environment remains largely untouched by leviathan blood. The massive walls and the great distance from the frontier ensure that the colossal creatures never reach this far inward. Forests, rivers, and farmland follow familiar patterns of growth and decay, creating a landscape that feels stable and predictable. Leviathan blood does exist here, but only in its most refined and carefully processed forms, brought inward as rare medicines, experimental compounds, or luxury elixirs for scholars and the wealthy. To most people in the Inner Tier, the strange biological landscapes of the outer regions are distant stories from the frontier. The rare places where leviathans have fallen in the Middle Tier are known about, but they feel remote, almost mythic, far removed from everyday life within the calm and ordered heart of civilization.  
  ---

  ## III. The Source of Class Powers

In this world, "magic" and "martial prowess" are not separate from biology. They are the human body evolving and mutating to function in an unstable environment saturated with Leviathan blood.

* **Martial Classes (Fighters, Rogues, Barbarians, Monks):** Your body has adapted to high-stress environments. Your reflexes are nervous-system mutations, and rages are direct hormonal responses to the presence of Leviathan blood or environmental stimuli.  
* **Primal Classes (Druids, Rangers):** You have a biological resonance with the blood-soaked land. You do not simply serve nature; you command its hyper-growth and calm its aggression through instinctual connection.  
* **Arcane Classes (Artificers, Bards, Wizards, Sorcerers, Warlocks):** You have learned to channel or reshape the energy of the land, such as sound, flame, or flesh. Whether through rigorous study or natural mutation from exposure, you command the fundamental elements of the environment.  
* **Divine Classes (Clerics, Paladins):** You manifest the collective will of the people. Your abilities are the body's method of purifying the corrupted energy of the Tiers to protect and heal those around you.  
  ---

  ## **IV. The City: Brinegate**

Brinegate sits directly against the **Second Wall**, precisely where the Middle Tier ends and the Outer Tier begins. Its sole purpose is the maintenance of the wall and to act as a gate between the Middle and Outer Tier.

* **The Atmosphere:** The city is busy with people coming and going, an administrative hub, a gathering of many different people.  
* **The Culture:** People avoid speaking of Leviathans directly. Instead, they focus on the technicalities of survival: stress fractures, maintenance delays, and incoming weather.  
* **Control:** All traffic through the gate is logged, and everything returning from or going to the Outer Tier is strictly examined for contamination.  
  ---

  ## **V. The Landmark: The Last Sound Inn**

A reinforced tavern located near the inner-facing gate, serving as a hub for patrols, couriers, and adventuring parties.

* **The Name:** The inn is named after the **Horn** mounted above the bar—a brass system woven into the wall.  
* **The Omen:** If a Leviathan breaches the First Wall and threatens the Second, the horn will sound one long, mournful note. It is known as the "last sound" most would hear before the end.  
* **The Role of Players:** The inn is where problems that the government deems too small or "not worthy of a desk" find their way to adventuring parties for coin and wanderlust.

# One-shot summary

# One-shot summary

* The party first met in the **Last Sound Inn** in Brinegate. During the evening, a veteran adventurer named **Kisara Solis** entered through the gate from the Outer Tier and quietly approached them with a task. She asked them to deliver a sealed package to a small graveyard where several of her former companions were buried. Unbeknownst to the group, a rival adventuring party overheard the conversation and began plotting to intercept them.  
* To reach the graveyard, the party had to travel along the **Wall** itself—an area both dangerous and strictly forbidden. Along the way they encountered a watchtower staffed by three guards. Rather than fight, the party relied on clever trickery: illusion magic, distractions, and even the unexpected help of squirrels allowed them to slip past unnoticed.  
* Further along the Wall, they encountered a mass of hostile, animated vines violently attacking the stone. Instead of destroying them, the group used calming music to pacify the plants. The moment nearly turned disastrous when the party’s tiefling paladin became entangled and badly injured before the others managed to pull him free.  
* When the party finally reached the graveyard, they found **Kisara already waiting among the graves**. Something felt wrong. Their suspicions proved correct: the figure was actually a wizard from the rival party using illusion magic to impersonate her in order to steal the package. Moments later, the real Kisara arrived and revealed the truth—this entire mission had been a **test**.  
* Kisara had grown tired of the endless dangers of the Outer Tier and wished to retire from adventuring. Instead, she hoped to establish a **new adventuring guild in Brinegate**, and she wanted to see if the party possessed the courage, ingenuity, and compassion necessary to become its founding members.  
* The rival party soon arrived, hoping to seize the prize for themselves. A fight broke out in the graveyard. Two of the rivals were slain, while the remaining two were convinced to surrender and abandon the conflict.  
* Inside the package was Kisara’s true reason for the test: a strange **mycelial mushroom**, capable of allowing her to commune with—and potentially revive—the spirit of her fallen lover and former companion. Yet such a revival would not restore him as he once was; it would only bring back a distorted and decaying echo of the man she loved.  
* The party spoke with Kisara at the graveside and gently persuaded her to let the past rest. With their support, she chose to release her lover’s memory rather than cling to it.  
* Together, they returned to Brinegate. There, with the party at her side, **Kisara began the work of founding a new adventuring guild**—one that would face the dangers of the Wall and the Outer Tier together.

# Wynn

*Player: Ruriko | Race: Forest Gnome | Class: Bard — College of Lore*

- Wynn is several centuries old — she doesn't know exactly how many. She has travelled most of the known world, gathering stories, songs, and the kind of knowledge that only comes from outliving everyone around you. Gnomes can live extraordinary lives, but most haven't: centuries of Leviathan battles have made them a vanishingly rare people, and Wynn carries the weight of that loss quietly.  
- For the past few years she has settled in Brinegate, playing music at the Last Sound Inn and supporting the artistic community — a profession neglected by a civilization perpetually at war. She has never fought the Leviathans directly. Her contribution has always been different: keeping the stories, the songs, and the culture alive so that there is something worth defending.  
- She took a liking to Mara, Querk, and Nyssara when they arrived a few months ago, and she became the informal thread that drew them together as a party. When Kisara needed people she could trust, Marlowe — the inn's waiter and Wynn's close friend — suggested Wynn and her new companions. They passed the test, and the Solis Lantern was born.  
- Wynn is the only party member who has been to the Outer Tier before, and one of the few people alive with a living memory of how the Empire and its environment have changed across centuries of Leviathan conflict.  
- Her greatest ongoing struggle is her age. She has loved and lost more people than she can easily count, and she carries that grief as a kind of background weight — not debilitating, but ever-present.

NPCs Wynn knows:

- **Thorvald Royce**: Human, 50s. Innkeeper of the Last Sound Inn. Broad-shouldered, few words, mechanical prosthetic arm — lost the original to wall work. Has employed Wynn for years and lets her stay at the inn without charge. Knows most of what moves through Brinegate, and has a quiet soft spot for her.

NPCs everyone know:

- **Kisara Solis** — Human, 50s, she/her. A veteran adventurer with decades of Outer Tier experience behind her and the scars to show for it. She retired not out of defeat but out of choice — she had seen enough, lost enough, and decided the most useful thing she could do was build something that outlasts her. The Solis Lantern is that thing. She runs it with the same directness she brought to adventuring: clear expectations, genuine investment in the people around her, and very little patience for wasted time. She is not warm in an obvious way, but the party will come to understand that her attention is a form of care.  
- **Marlowe Dei** — Human-ish, 20s, he/them/she. Former waiter and cook at the Last Sound Inn, now the Solis Lantern's secretary and butler, a role they have taken to with considerable enthusiasm. Marlowe moves through a room like they're dancing to music nobody else can hear, talks to plants as a matter of course, and has a gift for making people feel at ease without quite knowing how they do it. They are better connected to the quiet undercurrents of Brinegate than their cheerful demeanor suggests — people talk around Marlowe because they forget to be guarded.

Intro to session 1

You've called Brinegate home for years now, earning your keep at the Last Sound Inn, playing for travelers, locals, anyone who needed a song and a drink to get through the night. You know the city's rhythms, its faces, its moods. You've seen wet seasons come and go.

What's new is the company.

Over the past three months, the people you drew into your orbit have become something more than acquaintances. The military flows the other direction, pulled outward toward the coast, toward whatever is coming. Brinegate braces itself, as it always does.

This is where your next chapter begins.

# Querk

*Player: Sira | Race: Wood Elf | Class: Druid — Circle of the Moon*

*Pronouns: they/them*

- Querk grew up in an indigenous tribe deep in the forests of the Middle Tier, largely removed from the Empire's civilization. Her world changed when Tristan Bas, a linguist anthropologist, came to live with the tribe for several months. He befriended Querk, told her about the wider world, and left something unresolved between them — feelings that seemed mutual but were never spoken aloud before he returned to society.  
- In the past few years, two things have gone wrong. The environment around the tribal village has shifted in ways that feel unnatural, and Querk's mother Jenevele — the tribe's matriarch — has fallen ill with a sickness that no traditional remedy can touch. Querk stayed as long as she could, helping care for her mother alongside her younger sister Chi-Ro. Eventually she made the difficult decision to leave, telling herself it was to understand what was happening to the land. The quieter truth is that she also hopes to find Tristan again.  
- Tristan once mentioned working at a research facility called Jakata in the Outer Tier, near Brinegate. That became her destination. She arrived in Brinegate three months ago and has been stuck here since — indigenous people face a complicated legal status under Imperial law, and Querk is caught in a bureaucratic process to obtain citizenship before she can even apply for gate access. She joined the Solis Lantern partly for practical support, and partly because having people around her makes the waiting bearable.  
- Her sister Chi-Ro resents her for leaving. Her mother is sick and getting no Imperial healthcare. And somewhere beyond the wall, Tristan may hold answers to all of it.  
- **\[GM only\]** The tribal village where Querk grew up contains ancient markings and oral traditions in a language so old it has no known origin. Tristan was drawn there specifically because of these — his research into the tribe's linguistics was never purely academic. The cipher he eventually acquired is rooted in this language, and Querk, having grown up surrounded by it, may be the only living person who carries an instinctive familiarity with its patterns — without knowing that she does.

# Mara Embervale

*Player: Kirsi | Race: Tiefling (red-skinned) | Class: Paladin — Oath of Vengeance*

* Mara grew up close to the world of Leviathan research — her mother Alicent is today a Head Researcher of Leviathan Biology, stationed in the Outer Tier, but growing up in the Middle Tier, the proximity to her mother’s research shaped Mara in ways she is still working out. Her older sister Elody followed Alicent into science. Mara did not, though she carries her mother's intensity in a different form.  
* A few months ago, Alicent stopped answering letters. No explanation, no warning — just silence. When Elody went to find her, she too went quiet, her last letter saying only that she would soon be arriving in Brinegate. She never did.  
* Mara came to Brinegate three months ago to find them. She has pushed every door available to her — the research facility, the city government, the gate authorities — and has been met with indifference at every turn. No records. No answers. Nobody willing to say anything useful. The wall between the Middle and Outer Tier has been an immovable obstacle, and the wet season tightening gate access has made it worse.  
* She joined the Solis Lantern because going it alone had stopped working, and because she needed something to belong to while she figures out her next move. The guild gives her purpose. The party gives her people. But underneath both is the same question she arrived with: where are they, and what happened to them.  
* Mara has always struggled with her temper. Not ordinary frustration or short-fused irritability — something deeper and harder to control, episodes of rage that overtake her completely and require outside help to pull her back from. She has learned to read the warning signs, and the people close to her have learned them too. It is one of the reasons she values the party: not just for what they can do, but because they are people she trusts to bring her back when she goes too far. She carries some shame about it, and a quiet fear that one day she won't come back at all.

**\[GM only\]** 

* As a child, Alicent quietly experimented on Mara using refined Leviathan blood, without Mara's knowledge or consent. The episodes of overwhelming, uncontrollable rage Mara experiences are a direct result — a biological echo of the aggression Leviathans display during the wet season. This is not a coincidence. The research group's central breakthrough is the hypothesis that Leviathan aggression during the wet season follows a pattern that can be read and potentially soothed — and Mara, as a living test subject, is the closest thing to proof they have. Alicent's letter referring to her "hot-headed daughter" being integral to the research was not a metaphor.   
* The shadow group's confusion between Mara and Elody has so far kept Mara out of their hands — but once they understand who Alicent actually meant, that changes.   
* The full truth goes further. Alicent did not simply experiment on Mara as a child. She began before Mara was born, injecting herself with refined Leviathan blood while pregnant, so that the blood would shape Mara's biology from the very beginning. Mara was not a later subject, she was the intended one. Her entire physiology was designed around this. The rage episodes are not a side effect; they are the point. Alicent needed a living subject whose biology mirrored Leviathan aggression closely enough to test whether the cipher language could calm it. Mara is that subject. This has a consequence for the campaign finale: when the party has assembled the cipher, the functioning armor, and Wynn's memory of the sounds, they will still need to calibrate the language before using it on a Leviathan. The only way to do that is to test it on someone whose biology responds the same way. Mara is the only such person. She will be asked — or will offer — to let the party speak the cipher to her first, in order to learn whether it works and how. This requires her to consent to being used in exactly the way her mother used her without consent. Whether that choice feels like reclamation or repetition is for Kirsi to decide.

# Nyssara Hylach

*Player: Sine | Race: Half-Drow / Half-High Elf (appears fully Drow, golden eyes) | Class: Rogue | Alignment: Lawful Neutral*

- Nyssara is the illegitimate daughter of Dolores Hylach, a powerful Drow matriarch and one of the Empire's senior government ministers. Her father was a human soldier whose prolonged exposure to Leviathan blood during a previous wet season left a lasting mark on his biology — and on hers. Dolores chose him deliberately, less out of affection than calculation: she wanted to see what Leviathan blood would produce in the next generation. The golden eyes Nyssara inherited are the most visible result, and the one thing that could expose her origins to the Drow noble houses, who take blood purity seriously. She conceals them behind tinted glasses, telling anyone who asks that she has a light sensitivity condition. She doesn't. Her half-high-elf eyes handle light perfectly well.  
- Dolores has never treated Nyssara with anything but love — more, by most accounts, than she shows her many legitimate children. Whether that affection is maternal or proprietary is a question Nyssara has never asked aloud. What she knows is that her mother opened certain doors, kept certain secrets, and raised her in comfort. She also knows she will inherit nothing: not title, not estate, not formal standing. What she has instead is the family name and a mind that understood early that information is the one currency that doesn't require inheritance.  
- She collects it carefully and shares it sparingly. She builds relationships the same way — not coldly, but deliberately, always aware that the people around her today may be exactly what she needs tomorrow. The Solis Lantern was an obvious move: a founding membership means influence, agency, and a legitimate base from which to build an information network close to the Outer Tier, where the military and other high-standing individuals move through constantly. The guild is infrastructure. The adventures are access.  
- She is not running from her family so much as building something independent of it. The distinction matters to her, even if the result looks the same from the outside. Her mother writes often. Nyssara replies selectively.

### **What the Party Finds**

**The side door and signs of struggle**

**The scattered papers**

*A. Embervale. T. Bas.*

Below them, a single underlined phrase: *the biology responds to the words.*

*Let that land. Don't explain it. Move on.*

**The hidden compartment**

The shadow group searched this room thoroughly — but not thoroughly enough.

**Investigation DC 14** — A section of the floor beneath the heaviest workbench produces a subtly different sound when walked over. The bench itself shows faint scratch marks on the floor around its legs, suggesting it has been moved before — deliberately, and more than once.

*Moving the bench requires a* **Strength DC 12** *check or two characters working together, in which case no check is needed.*

Underneath: a flush panel with a small recessed latch, invisible unless you are looking for it. The compartment is sealed and undisturbed.

Inside are two items.

### **The Armor Prototype**

A breastplate, roughly fashioned, clearly a prototype rather than a finished piece. It is heavier than it looks and faintly warm to the touch in a way that has nothing to do with the temperature of the room.

**Arcana DC 12** — The warmth is not incidental. There is something active in the metal — not a spell exactly, more like a spell trying to complete itself. It is unfinished in a way that feels deliberate, as though it is waiting for something.

**Artificer or Investigation DC 13** — Along the inner surface, worked into the metal rather than applied afterward, are more of the same symbols from the scattered papers. They are structural, not decorative — load-bearing in some sense that isn't purely physical. One symbol on the left pauldron mounting point is incomplete, the lines stopping mid-form as though Fentley was interrupted mid-work.

### **The Journal**

Small, leather-bound, water-stained at the corners. The first half is technical notes — materials, failures, iterations, the slow progress of someone building something they don't fully understand yet. The handwriting becomes more compressed and urgent toward the middle.

**Investigation DC 10** — Several pages have been torn out from the latter half of the journal. Not messily — carefully, as though removed by Fentley himself rather than by whoever searched the room. Whatever was on those pages, he didn't want it found.

*Fragment — mid-journal:*

*Fragment — later:*

*A.'s last message before silence: she says the biology and the language are the same thing. I don't know what that means. I don't think she expected me to. She said to keep the armor safe and wait.*

*Final entry, handwriting rushed:* 

*Someone has been asking about me at the gate. I don't know who. Keeping this where they won't find it. If someone finds this who isn't them — the armor isn't finished. Don't let it be finished by the wrong hands.*

### **The Shape of It**

Three things are now in the room that weren't connected before: an unfinished armor built around an ancient symbol sequence, a researcher named Alicent Embervale whose name is on Fentley's wall, and a cipher that apparently came from somewhere deep in the tribal forests of the Middle Tier.

Nobody in the room has the full picture. But the edges of it are visible.

**Intelligence DC 13** — The torn pages, the hidden compartment, the incomplete symbol — Fentley wasn't just hiding his work. He was controlling what could be found and by whom. He expected someone might come looking, and he prepared for it.

*Ask the party: what do they take with them? Do they keep searching? And do they go back to Kisara now — or do they think they can afford to wait?*

*"Get some rest."*

## **X. The Shortcut**

*Wynn's route between the Last Sound Inn and the guild is not the main road. She has a shortcut — a narrow passage between two older buildings, poorly lit, barely wide enough for two people side by side. She has used it for years. It cuts nearly ten minutes off the walk, which matters less than the fact that it is quiet, and Wynn has always preferred quiet streets late at night after an evening of noise.*

The party finds the passage without much difficulty — anyone who knows Wynn's habits, or thinks to ask Thorvald or Marlowe, will be pointed here.

*Read the following when they enter the passage:*

The lane is dark enough that a light source helps. It is also still — the kind of still that a city produces at this hour, when most of the noise has gone to bed. The cobblestones are slightly damp from earlier in the evening.

Halfway down the passage, something catches the light.

**Perception DC 10** — A small shape on the ground, against the left wall. Dark, motionless.

It is a bird. Small, unremarkable, one wing folded against its body and the other — missing, the socket long healed over, an old wound on a creature that has lived with it for years. It is dead. Not from a fall or illness — its neck is broken, cleanly, the way something gets broken when it is in the way.

*Give the party a moment with this. They all know this bird.*

**Investigation DC 11** — Scuff marks on the cobblestones around the bird, consistent with a struggle that was brief and efficient. Wynn resisted. It didn't help much, and it wasn't meant to — this was over quickly.

**Investigation DC 13** — The scuff pattern suggests at least three people were involved besides Wynn. Two holding, one managing. Professional. They knew where she would be and they were ready.

**Perception DC 14** — On the wall at shoulder height, a small smear — not blood, something darker and slightly iridescent, like ink or dye. The kind of mark a gloved hand might leave against stone. It means nothing by itself. It might mean something later.

**Survival DC 13** — The marks lead toward the far end of the passage and then simply stop at the street beyond. Whatever they used to move her — a cart, a covered litter, something — was waiting. Tracking from here is not possible without more to go on.

There is nothing else. No note, no warning, no indication of who did this or why. Just the empty passage, the damp cobblestones, and the bird.

*Ask the party: what do they do right now, in this moment, before they think about next steps?*

*Let the answer be whatever it is. Then let them go back to the guild and tell Kisara.*

This is the first combat for some players. Keep a few principles in mind:

* **Make the hits feel real.** Describe what happens, not just the number. *"Your spell catches him across the shoulder and he stumbles back into the table, scattering cards across the floor."*  
* **Give Mara's rage a moment.** If Mara enters rage or deals significant damage, let it land with weight. She has been patient for a long time. This is the first time she gets to hit something that deserves it.  
* **Voss is the tactical wrinkle.** He will try to reach the back passage and escape if the fight turns against the group. Let the party decide whether stopping him is worth breaking from the main fight. If he escapes, the encounter consequence is that Otto learns the dungeon has been breached — which has implications for session 3\.
