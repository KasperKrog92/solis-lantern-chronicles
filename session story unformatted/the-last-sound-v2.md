# The Last Sound

---

The wet season is approaching.

You can feel it in Brinegate before you can see it, a tightening in the city's rhythms, a change in how people move and where they look. The gates to the Outer Tier carry twice their usual traffic as the last harvesters and researchers come back through, luggage heavier than when they left. Families move inland in ones and twos, making small calculations about distance. The military flows the other direction, organized columns heading toward the coast that the city has long since learned to step around without staring.

Nobody says the word Leviathan if they can help it. They don't need to.

The Last Sound Inn sits near the inner-facing gate, its outer wall built directly into the stone of the Middle Wall. On that wall hangs a series of brass pipes, a horn woven into the city's own infrastructure. If a Leviathan ever breaches the Outer Wall and turns toward Brinegate, the horn will sound. One long, mournful note carrying through every room and street and dream in the city.

They call it the last sound most people would ever hear.

This time of year, people come up to the pipes sometimes. They press a hand to the brass, neither prayer nor superstition, just the human need to touch something solid when the world feels uncertain. Then they step back and get on with it.

Brinegate is good at that. Getting on with it.

---

## The Inn

The common room is warm and loud on this particular morning, full of adventuring parties in various states of readiness and argument. Thorvald Royce, innkeeper, broad-shouldered, not given to unnecessary words, with a mechanical prosthetic arm that works the taps with practiced efficiency, moves behind the bar with his usual economy of motion. At the far end of the room, near a window that looks out toward the gate in the Wall, a small figure finishes the last notes of her set.

**Wynn** takes her bow.

She has been playing at the Last Sound for years, long enough that the inn feels like hers in the way only places do when you've been somewhere long enough to outlast everyone who knew you when you arrived. The crowd this morning is thinner than usual, people preoccupied in ways that don't invite music. She played anyway. She always plays anyway.

A small bird drifts down from the rafters as the last note fades, one wing folded properly, the other absent, the socket long since healed into a smooth, permanent curve. It makes lopsided circles above her head on the way to her shoulder, its small body listing beautifully to one side with every loop, describing the same imperfect arc it always does. Someone watching might think it's a flaw. Wynn has always thought it looks like dancing.

At a table near the window, three others wait. They have ordered food. They have left a portion for her. A small thing that adds up, over months, into something that deserves a name.

**Mara** sits closest to the window, watching the gate with the focus of someone who has been doing this long enough to know it probably won't work today. She is a tiefling with red skin and the stillness of a person who has learned to contain something that does not want to be contained. She is always looking for two faces. She has not found them yet.

**Nyssara** is at the same table, composed and deliberate, her tinted glasses in place. She watches the room the way she watches most rooms, as a collection of information waiting to be organized. The glasses are dark enough to conceal the color of her eyes. They do their job well.

**Querc** sits with both hands around something warm, a small drum worn smooth with use hanging at their hip. A wood elf with wildness underneath the stillness, the kind of person who has learned to hold a great deal of themselves in a small space. They have been watching Wynn play the way you watch something that makes you feel, briefly, less far from home.

They have been a party for a few months. They have run a few jobs together. Nothing that changed the world, but enough to know each other's rhythms, each other's silences, which arguments are worth having and which are better absorbed over breakfast.

Wynn joins them. The bird settles on her shoulder. Thorvald, unprompted, brings a drink to the end of the bar closest to her table.

That is when the door opens.

---

## Kisara

The inn does not go silent exactly. It changes register. Conversations continue at other tables, the bar keeps doing its business, but something in the room recalibrates, the way a room does when someone enters who has earned that from the world.

Kisara Solis moves carefully across the threshold. She is in her fifties, and the decades are on her, in the scars, in the careful way she favors her left side, in the directness with which she locates the table she wants and heads for it without pausing for the people who move toward her and then think better of it. Her leg has been torn open. She waves off the medics and concerned regulars with the authority of someone who has survived worse and does not require assistance saying so.

Thorvald has a drink waiting before she reaches the bar. He sets it down without comment. Kisara takes it, carries it to a table with its back against the Wall, and sits.

Every adventuring party in the room becomes suddenly, sharply aware of being looked at.

She is one of the few adventurers with standing permission to travel to the Outer Tier. She has been doing it for decades. There are people in this inn who have been telling each other her stories for years. She looks at all of them now with the calm assessment of someone who has not yet found what she is looking for.

Her eyes move from table to table. Then they find Wynn's party and stay.

A brief exchange with Marlowe, the inn's waiter, young and bright-eyed, currently weaving between tables with the grace of someone who moves as though dancing to music nobody else can hear. He leans down when Kisara speaks to him. He listens. He glances once, quickly, toward the table near the window, and then goes to fetch them.

---

## The Table

They approach her, a little quickly perhaps, trying not to look like they approached quickly. Kisara watches them cross the room with the expression of someone conducting an evaluation she has already half-completed.

She does not offer pleasantries. She produces a wrapped package, sealed and plain, sets it on the table between them.

*"I need this transported. A graveyard, two days north — Thornhaven Cemetery. I would take it myself."* She glances at her leg. *"I did the hard part. I just need someone to take it the rest of the way."*

The party asks questions. The right ones, as it happens. She answers some of them.

She does not say what is in the package.

She does not say because she does not know. Darrow left her a last task and she followed it, because that is what you do when someone you loved asks you to do something after they are gone. The details belong to the task, not to the person carrying it out.

Querc asks if they can look at her leg. There is a moment, Kisara assessing whether this is worth the indignity, and then she extends it.

*[Persuasion check — Querc succeeds.]*

Querc's hands find the wound with practiced ease. The flesh knits slowly, not all the way, Kisara does not stay still quite long enough for that, but enough. Kisara looks down at the result without comment, which from her counts as acknowledgment.

At the far end of the table, Nyssara's glasses slip.

*[Perception check — Natural 1.]*

Her amber eyes are exposed for half a second before she adjusts them. She scans the room quickly, looking for anyone who might have seen. She registers nothing of concern.

She has, however, missed something concerning entirely.

---

## The Cockroach

Querc sees it first.

A cockroach on the leg of a nearby table, small, unremarkable, the kind of creature a city inn produces in quantity. Querc watches it for a moment with the instinct of someone who has spent enough time in the natural world to notice when something in it is wrong.

The cockroach is not moving. Not in the way cockroaches are still when they are waiting or feeding, but in the way a well-made thing is still when it is receiving.

*[Insight check — Querc succeeds.]*

It is mechanical. Crystallized, constructed, articulated to look organic. It is not watching the room the way insects watch rooms. It is *watching* the room.

The moment Querc leans toward it, it moves, quickly, with a purpose no natural cockroach would have, threading between chair legs toward a specific table across the room. Querc glances at Wynn, who is mid-sentence with Kisara. Glances at the others.

Then Querc becomes a mouse.

---

The table the cockroach belongs to holds four people who have not been looking at Kisara, which is its own kind of tell. They are the Gilt Knives, a mercenary crew with the look of people who have been almost successful for long enough to develop strong opinions about it. Their leader, a human woman called Senna, sits with the coiled posture of someone who has been passed over for opportunities she considers hers by right. Beside her, a goblin in an oversized coat covered in pockets, Fetch, young and brilliant and watching the room with eyes that have gone slightly glassy, slightly still.

Those eyes clear the moment the cockroach returns to him. Fetch blinks rapidly, coming back into himself. His construct has delivered its report.

Mouse-Querc watches him absorb it.

Fetch knows about the package. About Thornhaven. About the terms.

The party, when Querc returns to their elf form and tells them, looks to Kisara. Kisara shrugs, the way of someone who has factored this in already.

*"It's part of the job,"* she says. *"If it were easy, I wouldn't need to hire anyone."*

She looks at them the way she has been looking at them since they sat down, waiting to see what people are made of.

Then she tells them about the route.

---

## The Road

There are two ways to Thornhaven.

Through the forest: longer, safer in most senses. About three days. Through the Wall road: faster, direct, two days at pace. Also illegal. The Wall is critical infrastructure, patrolled and maintained, and traveling alongside it without authorization carries consequences.

*"I'd take the Wall,"* Kisara says. She does not suggest. She recommends. The distinction is subtle but present.

The party discusses it. Kisara watches the discussion the way a teacher watches a test, not intently enough to seem invested, but not looking away either. She says nothing.

They choose the Wall.

She tells them, before they go, about where they are headed. Thornhaven Cemetery holds adventurers, hundreds of them over the years, the people who went into dangerous places and did not come back, or came back changed, or came back and then went out again until the third or fourth time when they did not. Three specific graves she wants them to find when they arrive.

Darrow Thane. Mira Sweetwater. Kess Ironfinger.

Darrow died three months ago. Mira and Kess died five years back, in the last Leviathan breach of the Outer Wall. They helped fight it back. They didn't survive the fight.

*"I was supposed to be there,"* Kisara says, and does not elaborate, and the party understands that this sentence contains a great deal that is not being said. She moves on before anyone can ask.

The party says goodbye to Marlowe, who gives them each something from the kitchen in a cloth pouch and tells them to be careful with an earnestness that makes it feel like more than a figure of speech. They gather their gear. They step out into the pre-wet-season city and head north toward the Wall.

---

## The Guard Tower

The Middle Wall is enormous in the way that things built over centuries to solve a problem civilization cannot afford to fail at are enormous. Up close, the stonework is stratified, you can see the different eras in the masonry, the repairs and reinforcements, two hundred years of maintained terror. The road alongside it is well-kept. The road is also, at intervals, watched.

Less than an hour of walking, and Nyssara holds up a hand.

*[Perception check — Nyssara succeeds.]*

A guard tower, built into the base of the Wall itself: two guards on the ground scanning the forest to the west, and one above with a spyglass sweeping the road in both directions. The road runs straight past it. There is no cover, and going around through the forest would cost time they have made a choice not to spend.

The party considers the tower from sufficient distance that they have not yet been seen. Plans are proposed and weighed and found wanting, revised, proposed again. Eventually a shape emerges, not a plan exactly, more like a general theory of audacity.

Querc moves carefully into the tree line.

---

The squirrel sees the elf before the elf sees the squirrel.

It is sitting on a low branch watching Querc approach with the attention of a creature that has decided not to run yet but reserves the right to change its mind. Querc stops. They look at each other.

*"Is anyone brave enough,"* Querc asks, in the patient way they have of speaking to creatures, not louder, not slower, just the register of someone who takes the question seriously, *"to help us? There's something we need at the top of that tower."*

A pause. The squirrel considers this.

*"What something?"*

Querc glances at the tower. At the guard with the spyglass.

*"That."*

The squirrel looks at the spyglass. Looks at its friends in the branches behind it. Looks back at Querc.

*"What do we get?"*

*"The tower,"* Querc says, *"and everything in it. If you're strong enough to take it."*

A silence in which something is clearly being decided. Then the squirrel drops from the branch and lands in the leaves with the unmistakable posture of a creature that has just committed to something.

*"I'm Pebble,"* it announces. *"I'll do it."*

---

The approach is a small masterwork of collaboration and increasing chaos.

Nyssara raises her hands and tiny lights appear at the tree line, small flames moving through the underbrush in the pattern of torches held by running people. Mara extends a hand and the forest shudders, the ground trembling slightly, trees rustling in still air. And Wynn, lifting her instrument, produces the sound of voices from the dark, panicked, breathless, the shape of people fleeing something large and close.

The two ground guards exchange a look. Then they run into the forest, exactly as people whose job is to protect the Wall have been trained to do.

The guard above, with the spyglass, peers down at them going. Then he sweeps the road. Nothing. He sweeps the tree line. Nothing that isn't leaves.

Then a small shape appears at the base of the tower and begins to climb.

Pebble reaches the top.

What happens between Pebble and the guard is not entirely visible from a distance, but it involves a great deal of frantic motion on the guard's part and a great deal of focused determination on the squirrel's, and then a great deal of additional squirrels arriving from the direction of the tree line with the collective energy of creatures who have just been told there is a tower available for occupation.

*"Run,"* Nyssara says.

Wynn's legs are short, but she is already moving. Mara, with the ease of someone who has made a quick calculation and arrived at an obvious answer, scoops Wynn off the ground mid-sprint. Wynn makes a noise somewhere between protest and delight. They run. They are not, by any measure, stealthy. They are spectacularly loud for a group that started this plan with the word *stealth*, all clattering armor and gnome-and-tiefling logistics and Nyssara's expression of refined exasperation as she runs alongside them with considerably more dignity than the situation merits.

The tower guard hits the ground.

Not dead. Dazed, winded, surrounded by squirrels who are already conducting a detailed survey of their new home. From a safe distance, the party slows to a walk, breathing hard and largely triumphant.

They hear a small *tik-tik* of the spyglass being picked up.

Querc looks back.

Pebble, perched at the top of the tower, has the spyglass to one eye. He trains it carefully on the retreating party. Even at this distance, you can tell he is pleased with himself.

The party keeps walking. The squirrels settle in.

---

## The Vines

Some hours later, the forest sends a reminder that the Middle Tier is not as safe as it appears on maps.

The path ahead ends.

Not abruptly, the change builds over a hundred yards, the forest pressing closer to the Wall, the undergrowth thickening and then becoming something else entirely. Vines. Enormous, layered, dozens of them twisting up from the forest floor and slamming into the Wall with rhythmic, furious force. Stone chips lie scattered across the road. The Wall has taken damage here, cracks across centuries-old masonry, places where the face has been stripped completely. The vines are ongoing. They are not stopping.

The party stands at the edge of this and considers it.

Querc steps closer and reaches for the sense that connects them to the land, the living biological pulse of it, the network of root and soil and blood-soaked earth that runs through the whole of the Middle Tier in ways that are not always visible.

What they find is anger.

*[Nature check — Querc succeeds.]*

The vines are blood-touched, evolved by the Leviathan ichor that saturates this land, changed in ways that have made them hot, aggressive, and furious in the manner of things that have been made into weapons by forces they cannot understand. The Wall emanates heat, always has, the residual warmth of the infrastructure. To these vines, it reads as threat. They have been hitting it for days. They will keep hitting it. They don't know how to stop.

Querc tells the others what they found. There is a pause while everyone considers the vines and the vines continue to not notice anyone.

Then Wynn sits down, takes out her instrument, and begins to play.

---

The music takes a moment to reach them.

A slow piece, careful, patient, the kind of melody that asks nothing from the listener except attention. Querc pulls the small drum from their hip and finds the rhythm in it, something older and lower, the kind of beat that comes from a tradition that understood the relationship between sound and the land long before the Empire started building walls to manage it.

The vines slow.

Not all at once, gradually, the way agitation drains out of a creature when it stops feeling under threat. The frantic beating against the Wall becomes something less desperate. The air, which had felt charged, settles slightly. The music continues, and the vines, by degrees, listen.

*[Performance check — Wynn and Querc succeed.]*

They are calmer. They are not gone. They still fill the road entirely, a curtain of living anger at rest.

Mara walks up to the vines and puts her hand on them.

Something moves through her that she will think about later, maybe. The feeling is specific: not threat, not even pain, but recognition, the recognition of a thing she knows from the inside. Something that gets very hot and cannot cool itself down. Something that hits because it cannot think of anything else to do. Something that would stop if it could, if there were only something outside it capable of saying *you can stop now*.

*[Insight check — Mara succeeds.]*

She is the wrong person for this, probably. She runs hotter than most. But the vines feel the familiarity back, whatever it is that passes between two things that have the same problem and recognize each other through it. They do not lash out at her hand.

She takes hold of the nearest vine, careful and firm, and pulls it upward. Then the next one. Gradually, working with a steadiness that costs her something, she opens a gap in the wall of vegetation, not large, but passable, a narrow parting in the green.

Nyssara moves.

She assesses the gap with the look of someone calculating dimensions and acceptable risk, and then she goes through it in a single fluid motion, hands slightly raised, not touching anything. She lands on the other side and turns back with the composed expression of someone who has done the sensible thing and is now waiting for the others.

She had not entirely calculated that the others would have more trouble.

Wynn and Querc start through the gap, still playing as they go, and the vines feel the contact of two warm bodies moving through them and begin, slowly, to tighten. Mara holds the opening with both hands. She can feel it getting harder. The vines want to close. The music is helping but the music cannot reach the vines that are touching people, and those vines have simpler instructions.

*This is going to be close.*

The moment arrives when Mara understands that Wynn and Querc are not going to make it through on their own.

She releases the vines.

She pushes them.

She gets her shoulder behind Wynn and her arm around Querc and she shoves, hard, and they stumble through onto the other side, and then the vines close around Mara entirely.

What the others see from the far side is one hand.

Mara's hand, extended from the wall of vines, fingers spread. And then, muffled through half a meter of vegetation, the sound of her voice.

---

Nyssara's rapier is out before the hand has stopped moving. She does not deliberate. She begins cutting.

On the other side, Wynn and Querc drop their instruments and grab the hand.

*[Athletics check — Mara pulls herself out.]*

She comes through hard, bleeding from a dozen vine-cuts, her armor scraped and dented in places where the vines were strong enough to work on metal. The cuts across her exposed skin are livid and numerous. Her clothes are in a state that suggests the vines had opinions about fabric. She is, by any reasonable accounting, in bad shape.

She is also upright, which is its own answer.

Querc's hands find her before she quite catches her breath. The healing is steady and practiced, the same instinct that tended Kisara's leg at the inn, the understanding of what a body needs and in what order. Wynn adds her own magic to it, quieter and warmer, the particular kindness of someone who has been caring for things for a very long time.

*[Healing spells — cast. Mara is stable.]*

Mara looks at each of them. She looks like she is about to say something practical. What comes out is softer.

*"Thank you,"* she says.

Querc says *"Thank you"* back, meaning it for different reasons.

Wynn says nothing, but puts her hand briefly against Mara's, and that is enough.

After a moment, someone thinks to check the package. It is on Mara's back, where it has been since Brinegate. The wrapping is intact. Whatever is inside is fine.

Nobody opens it. Kisara asked them not to.

They walk on.

---

## Night

The forest finds them a campsite before dark, a small clearing with the quality of a place that has been undisturbed long enough to feel genuinely peaceful, something increasingly rare as you get closer to the Wall. They build a fire. They eat. The day settles around them slowly.

Querc disappears briefly into the edge of the trees and comes back with mushrooms.

A specific variety, something from home, grown in the kind of soil that exists near places where the land remembers being older than the Empire. Querc's people use them in certain rituals, the kind you do before something uncertain, when you need your dreams to tell you what your waking mind cannot reach.

*"They have a calming effect,"* Querc says. *"And they help you see things that might help tomorrow."*

The fire crackles. Somewhere above, [BIRD] makes a small sound and settles deeper into the pouch on Wynn's pack.

*"I'm in,"* says Mara.

One by one, they are all in.

Querc boils the water. The mushrooms steep. The package on the ground nearby begins, softly, to glow, organic light, pulsing slightly in rhythm with something none of them can name. They notice it. They look at it. They decide, together and without saying so, to honor the terms of the errand.

*"Kisara asked us not to,"* Mara says, and that is the end of that.

They drink the tea around the dying fire, watching the first stars appear, and then one by one the night takes them.

---

## Dreams

*Mara* stands at the end of a corridor and her family walks toward her, her mother Alicent, her sister Elody, both of them luminous and whole, arms open. She moves toward them and they burn. Not suddenly, not violently, they burn the way candles do, starting at the edges, the light consuming itself. Her mother speaks through the flame:

*"You were not quick enough. Your fire killed us."*

And Elody, already ash at the shoulders, turns eyes on her that are not accusing but desperate, pleading, something more complex than blame, and says:

*"I will be the hot-headed one. Just stay safe."*

The ash falls. Mara is on her knees in a corridor that is now empty and she is making a sound she cannot stop.

---

*Wynn* walks through a world with no one left in it.

Not a ruined world, a peaceful one, forests and rivers and the kind of sky that makes you remember how old the sky actually is. She is the last. She has been the last before, in the way that something that has lived long enough eventually is. She opens her mouth and no sound comes out.

She listens instead. The trees have a song. The wind carries harmonics she has been trying to name for centuries. The animals of the forest move through patterns that are themselves a kind of music, and she sits down in the middle of all of it and it fills her completely, vast and enough.

She is the only person left and the world is still singing and she finds, with some surprise, that she is at peace.

---

*Nyssara* is young again, in a room she knows.

The drow across the table from her has been her teacher for years, patient and precise and entirely devoted, so he has always seemed, to her success and safety. He is explaining something. The nature of information. The nature of families. The nature of what it means to carry a secret.

*"Your eyes could ruin everything,"* he says, with the gentleness of a person who loves you enough to tell you hard truths. *"If people find out what you are, your mother will have no choice. But she won't find out. Because I will make sure she doesn't. I will always be here. I won't abandon you, even if the truth comes out."*

He reaches across the table and adjusts her glasses.

*"We just have to make sure it doesn't."*

His smile is warm. His smile is the smile of a man who is helping you. This is what helping looks like. This is what being safe feels like.

*This is what being managed feels like,* some part of her thinks, in the dream, and does not yet know what to do with the thought.

---

*Querc* runs.

Not from anything, just the pure fact of it, four legs on earth, the forest moving past on either side at the speed of something that belongs to the forest. They are a wolf, and they are perfect, and the world is exactly the right size for what they are. There is no past in this body. No sickness in a village far away, no mother's face growing thinner in the firelight, no anthropologist who said the things he said and then left, no citizenship process stuck in Imperial bureaucracy, no body that has never quite felt like the right shape.

There is only forward. There is only now. The pack is ahead, somewhere, and the night is everything.

They could stay here.

They are aware of being able to stay here in a way that is not hypothetical. The forest stretches on in all directions. The human, the elf, the person with the problems and the grief and the wrong body: they could leave that person at the edge of the trees.

*[Wisdom saving throw — DC 18. Querc succeeds.]*

It takes a long time. The pull is real and Querc understands why animals who could become people never do, there is freedom in the not-knowing, in the total absence of the question of who you are. But Querc is the matriarch's firstborn, and the matriarch's firstborn has a drum they carved at twelve years old, and there is a woman sick with an unnamed illness, and there is a man somewhere beyond a Wall who went away with a thing he did not fully explain, and Querc has not been able to forgive themselves for leaving and will not be able to until they have done something that makes the leaving worth it.

They become an elf again at the edge of a dream.

They wake first, before the others, and sit in the ember-glow watching the fire breathe until the world comes back.

---

## Morning

They wake in stages, and when they are all awake they do not immediately speak.

The silence is charged at first, the rawness of having been seen, of having glimpsed in each other's faces something that was not meant to be observed. They all know something now that they did not know when they fell asleep, or rather they feel it, which is both less and more than knowing. The embarrassment of this is real and nobody is quite sure what to do with it.

Then Querc puts a pot over the rekindled fire and Nyssara, without comment, finds what is left of the food in her pack and sets it out, and the ritual of breakfast begins to do what breakfast rituals do, absorb the difficulty, give the hands something to work on, allow the silence to become something less charged by degrees.

By the time they eat, the silence is different. The kind where people look at each other across a fire and the look contains more than either of them has said and neither of them needs to say it.

They pack their things. They check the package, still intact, still glowing faintly in a way that is easier to ignore in daylight.

They are more like a team than they were yesterday. They will be more like a team tomorrow. This is how it works.

---

## The Second Day

The Wall road is easier going on the second day, the ground firmer, the forest less aggressive. The party moves with the settled rhythm of people who have survived one difficult day and adjusted their expectations accordingly.

Nyssara, somewhere around midmorning, stops without breaking stride.

Her eyes have gone to the ridge behind them. A glance, smooth, casual, designed to look like nothing.

*[Perception check — Nyssara succeeds, wildly.]*

For a fraction of a second, at the crest of a low hill, she saw the top of a head. Dark hair, good coat. Gone behind the ridge before she could be certain.

She tells the party. They discuss, briefly, the possibility of doubling back and setting an ambush, and then file it away under *unnecessary problems* and keep walking. They have a destination. Whoever is behind them has their own agenda.

They pick up the pace.

---

## Thornhaven

The sun is low when they arrive, and the light it casts through the cemetery gates is the gold of late afternoon in early autumn, flattering to stone, turning even the oldest markers into something that looks almost warm.

Thornhaven is not a small cemetery. Adventurers die in quantity and their people put them somewhere, and this is somewhere, and it has been accumulating residents for generations. The markers range from elaborate carved stone to simple wooden posts, and the whole place has the quality of a graveyard that has received a great deal of grief and absorbed it rather than being diminished by it.

At the center, a figure stands among the graves.

The party stops at the gate.

*[Mara, Perception check — succeed.]*

Something is not right.

Kisara left Brinegate after them. She was injured. There is no route from the city that is faster than the Wall road. This cannot be Kisara.

---

They approach carefully and separately, or rather, one of them does not approach at all.

*[Nyssara, Stealth — succeed. Nyssara moves along the cemetery perimeter.]*

She drifts to the eastern edge of the grounds, silent and purposeful, and raises one hand.

Dancing lights rise, small soft points of light, floating on invisible currents, drifting toward the figure in the center of the cemetery with all the deliberate calm of a person who wants to see how something reacts.

Querc, meanwhile, becomes a snake.

*[Wild Shape — Querc transforms.]*

Snake-Querc moves through the grass of the cemetery with the invisibility of something low to the ground and not in a hurry. A long route, patient, letting the snake's senses do the work.

Near the central graves, the grass parts around a flat stone, and on the stone:

A cockroach.

*The same cockroach.*

Its crystallized carapace catches the dying light. Its crystallized eyes are aimed at the figure in the center of the grounds.

The figure, at that moment, makes an irritated gesture. The floating lights vanish.

The real Kisara did not appear to be a magic user.

Querc retreats through the grass. Nyssara is already circling back. The party convenes at the far wall of the cemetery and the shapes become clear: the Gilt Knives. Fetch, transformed, standing where Kisara should be. Renn and Kade somewhere deeper in the grounds, visible as shadows between the markers. Senna, their leader, nowhere obviously visible.

The party is discussing strategy when a hand falls on Mara's shoulder from behind.

They spin, weapons half-raised.

Kisara Solis looks back at them. Real, present, slightly out of breath, with the expression of someone who has been following people for two days and is not entirely surprised to have been caught.

*"You're better than I expected,"* she says, which from her is approximately a compliment.

*"You followed us,"* Nyssara says. Not a question.

*"I did the hard part,"* Kisara says, with a slight quirk at the corner of her mouth that is the closest thing to a smile she has offered since the inn. *"I wanted to see how you handled the rest."*

She looks at the grounds. She notes the fake Kisara at the central graves.

*"The real gnome is behind the fountain in the center,"* she says.

She hands Mara back the package.

---

## The Graveyard

What follows is the kind of situation that adventurers look back on and describe as *it all happened at once*, which is not quite true but captures the feeling accurately enough.

Nyssara and Wynn take the long way around, silent, ending up behind Renn and Kade with the unambiguous geometry of knives against backs. Renn, to his credit, immediately does the math.

Mara and Kisara walk straight up the center.

The Fetch illusion drops. Where Kisara was, there is a goblin in an oversized coat covered in pockets, watching the approaching party with the bright unreadable expression of someone who has decided they are going to get what they came for. He keeps looking at the package. Not at Mara. At the package, with the fixed attention of someone who has been thinking about a specific thing for a long time and now cannot see anything else.

Senna emerges from behind the fountain with two rapiers drawn, positioning herself between Mara and everything, the look of a soldier who has decided this is her moment.

*"The package,"* she says. *"That's all we want."*

*"No,"* Mara says.

The silence that follows contains a great deal of information.

Fetch begins building something between his hands. The light of it is not natural. The heat of it is real and getting realer.

Querc is a snake no longer.

*[Wild Shape — Querc transforms into a wolf.]*

The wolf covers the ground between Querc and Fetch with a speed that is not compatible with reaction time, and then it is not compatible with Fetch being upright, and then it is not compatible with Fetch being a problem anymore.

It is over quickly. It is not quiet.

Renn and Kade, behind them, hear it. The knives at their backs are already there. The arithmetic is simple.

*"We're not loyal to them,"* Renn says, the same voice from the market, the same recalculating quality. *"It was just work."*

*"Then go,"* Nyssara says.

They go. Quickly. Into the tree line without looking back.

That leaves Senna.

Senna, who is looking at what remains of Fetch, and at the wolf, and at the blade Mara has not put away, recalculating something that involves very different variables than she started the evening with. She raises her rapiers slowly. Lowers them slowly. She looks at Mara with an expression that has some real things in it, fear, and anger, and the resentment of someone who believes the world has been distributing its rewards unfairly.

*"That's illegal,"* she says, nodding at the package. *"Whatever's in there. Resurrection tech. I'll report it."*

*"You can try,"* Mara says.

Senna steps back. Steps back again. Reaches the cemetery gate and pauses with her back to it.

*"You'll regret this,"* she says.

Then she turns and walks away from the cemetery, into the long evening light.

She does not hear the wolf behind her until it is already too late to run.

---

The wolf brings her down at the edge of the cemetery, slowly, with a deliberateness that has nothing hurried in it, a thorn-vine emerging from a paw, winding around an ankle, dragging. Senna screams. The others stand at the cemetery entrance and watch.

No one moves to stop it.

Not because they approve, exactly. Not because they think this is what should happen. But there is something in the wolf that is beyond the question of should, and they can feel it, the loss of the line between the animal and the person, the thing that happens when you have given yourself over to something completely.

It ends.

The wolf stands in the grass, heaving, blood on its muzzle, on all fours over what is left of Senna. Then, slowly, the shape changes.

Querc is an elf again.

They are on their hands and knees in the grass, staring at their own hands. There is blood on their face. Their eyes have the quality of a person who has come back from somewhere very far and is not entirely sure of the distance yet.

The party waits.

Wynn crosses the grass and sits down next to them and puts her arms around them.

For a while, that is enough.

Eventually, someone searches Senna's body and finds a folded note in the breast pocket. No signature. A precise, unhurried hand:

*Bring me the resurrection technology Kisara carries and you will be rewarded handsomely.*

Someone knew what was in the package. Someone planned around it. The party looks at the note and then at each other and says nothing about what this means, because what this means is a question that will take longer than a graveyard evening to answer.

Kisara takes the note and reads it. Her face does nothing.

---

## Darrow

The package glows stronger near the graves.

Kisara opens it herself, standing at the center of Thornhaven where Darrow Thane's marker stands between Mira Sweetwater and Kess Ironfinger. The light from inside the wrapping spreads outward through the grass, finding the edges of the three graves like it knows where to go. The cemetery is lit in something softer than lantern light, organic and warm, the light of something that has been waiting.

Inside is a mushroom.

Not an ordinary mushroom, a mycelium construct, engineered and cultivated, glowing with its own internal luminescence. The same organism that was in the tea. The same organism that made their dreams bleed into each other last night.

Kisara takes it in both hands. It responds to the air around the graves, vibrating slightly, its glow cycling. And then the light rises.

It forms slowly, not light exactly, but the shape of light given the outline of a man. Tall, rangy, carrying himself with the ease of someone who has spent most of his life outdoors. The features come last: weathered, warm, familiar in the way that faces are familiar when they are the face of someone who has seen you through many versions of yourself.

His voice arrives before his mouth moves, a whisper from the light:

*"Kis."*

Kisara does not move. She is very still.

*"I'm glad you found it,"* Darrow says. *"I'm glad to see you."*

He looks at the party, gathered at the edge of the light. Something knowing in the look, gratitude and permission at once, the acknowledgment of people who came through and brought her here.

He turns back to Kisara.

*"We could have more time,"* he says. *"If you want it. There's still so much we didn't see."*

Kisara is on her knees.

This is the most surprising thing the party has seen in two days, not the vine wall or the squirrel or the wolf, but this. Kisara Solis, veteran of decades, finder of ways through impossible things, on her knees in the grass of a graveyard, holding a glowing mushroom, crying.

*"You're an idiot,"* she says, through tears, and the love in it is total.

She looks up at the party.

*"Tell me,"* she says. *"Tell me honestly."*

---

The party investigates what they can.

*[Multiple checks — Arcana, Medicine, Insight. The party succeeds at what they need to succeed at.]*

The mycelium can sustain something like a resurrection, but *like* is doing a great deal of work in that sentence. Whatever returned would not be Darrow. It would be a version of him, biological, degrading, a person losing themselves one memory at a time until the process finished and there was nothing left worth calling a person. Weeks, perhaps. Months at generous accounting.

She would get some time. She would watch him dissolve.

The party tells her this.

She already knew, in some part of herself she has been not-listening to for three months. Since the letter arrived. Since she started walking toward the Outer Tier.

She is silent for a long moment.

*"He would hate it,"* she says. *"Watching me watch him go."*

Wynn, who has lived long enough to know something about what it means to outlive people you loved completely, moves to where Kisara is kneeling and sits beside her.

*"It's hard,"* Wynn says. *"Letting go is always hard. But I have lived long enough to know this: the people we lose do not stay gone. They live in what we do. In who we make ourselves become. In the guilds we build."*

She says this last thing simply, without performance, and it lands exactly where it is meant to.

Kisara looks at her. Looks at all of them.

Then she turns back to the light.

---

## Goodbye

*"You're right,"* she tells him. *"You know you're right."*

His light is steady and patient.

*"I know."*

*"You came up with a resurrection scheme,"* she says. *"That's the stupidest thing you've ever done."*

*"I've done stupider."*

*"I can't think of one."*

*"The boar incident."*

*"The boar incident doesn't count."*

Darrow laughs. It sounds like wind through leaves, like something the world is making for her one more time out of whatever is left. She presses her hand against the light and the light presses back.

*"Kis,"* he says, softer. *"You were never alone. You just forgot how to let people show you."*

He looks at the party again. Something final in the look. Something like trust.

*"Go on,"* he says. *"You know what you're doing."*

Kisara deactivates the mushroom.

The light builds to one last flare of itself and then opens, a wave of memory washing through everyone present. They are not themselves for a moment. They are Darrow, seeing through his eyes: Kisara laughing by a fire, younger, her face unlined, Mira and Kess there, all of them whole and present and together. The warmth of it is not metaphorical. It is the warmth of a campfire in good company and the knowledge that you are exactly where you are supposed to be.

The memories come in flashes after that. Mira and Kess, gone in a wall of Leviathan aggression, the grief of two people watching what they could not prevent and holding each other in the aftermath. Years of Darrow and Kisara, older, sitting at the edge of things watching the sun go down. His hand finding hers. No words needed.

And last: three faces, young again, somehow, standing in bright sunlight. Darrow, Mira, Kess. Whole. Unhurried. Darrow turns and nods once.

Gratitude. Permission. Goodbye.

The graveyard returns to itself. Moonlight. Grass. Markers in the dark.

Kisara stays on her knees a while longer. The party stands in the dark and does not try to fill it.

---

## The Campfire

They build one at the edge of the cemetery, far enough from the graves to feel like they are giving the ground its space, close enough to feel like they are still part of it somehow. Food is found. Wood is gathered. The fire is larger and warmer than strictly necessary for a late autumn night, and nobody objects.

Kisara lifts her head and looks at the party across it.

*"The Solis Lantern,"* she says. *"That's what I'm calling it. The guild. I want you in it."*

She says it simply, the way she says everything, not asking for applause, not performing the moment. Just offering it.

*"You did the job,"* she says. *"You did it well. And you've got the makings of something. I know the makings when I see them."*

She looks at each of them in turn, which from her counts as ceremony.

*"Are you in?"*

---

They are in.

The fire settles into itself. Kisara begins to talk, not about the mission, not about logistics, not about anything that requires action. She talks about Darrow, about Mira, about Kess. She tells the story of the boar incident, which is apparently legendary among people who knew them, and which earns the closest thing to genuine laughter she has given them yet. She tells the story of Mira's cooking, which was distinguished primarily by its ambition and total indifference to success. She tells the story of Kess carrying her out of a burning building with one hand because Kess only needed one hand for that kind of thing.

She talks until the fire burns low, and then she lets it burn.

The note is still in her pocket. *Bring me the resurrection technology and you will be rewarded handsomely.* Someone knew what was in the package. Someone planned around it.

She will think about that in the morning.

Tonight, she sits with the people who helped her through it, and the fire does what fires do, and the Solis Lantern exists.

---

*They leave for Brinegate at dawn.*

*A new guild is waiting.*
